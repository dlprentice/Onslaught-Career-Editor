# The Battle Engine - Technology Lore

### The Aquila Program

The following is an in-universe memo from Tatiana Kiralova to High Command:

> **From**: Kiralova, Tatiana [tkiralova@research.army.gov.for]
> **To**: High Command
> **Cc**: Kramer, Chuck
> **Subject**: Aquila program results
> **Reference**: 14A883C

> The first Aquila prototype is ready to commence full and final testing. Although I have no doubt you are fully aware of the extents of the program, I have provided you with a brief overview of our design philosophy:

### Design Philosophy

> The Battle Engine program was designed to come up with a new type of war machine. It had to be **versatile, extremely mobile** and have the **firepower to get the job done**.

### Power System

> Advances in **clean fusion power** enabled the production of an extremely lightweight and small power source. This, in turn, allowed the overall weight of the machine to be kept to a minimum, which would be vital as the flight systems took shape.

### Walker Mode

> A basic walker design was chosen to allow for maximum mobility across a variety of terrains. Rather than tackle the balance issues associated with a two-legged design, the stability of **four legs** was settled on. This allows the Battle Engine to form a very secure base on which to deploy heavier weapons than would normally be possible on a wheeled or hovering chassis.

### Flight System

> Complementing the Walker mode, an advanced flight system allows the Battle Engine to pick and choose where it engages the enemy. Key to the flight system was the reduction of weight in the overall machine coupled with the diversion of energy systems to power the **anti-gravity thrusters and main engines**.

### Weapons

> The weapon systems have been specifically designed to be **modular in nature**, allowing a Battle Engine to be re-fitted to a variety of configurations in a matter of minutes. All weapons are based on existing Forseti technology although my department has managed to increase the yield of most of them to take advantage of the larger space available.

### Tatiana's Conclusion

> In short, provided the issue of pilot training can be resolved (see Memo TK:1703A), we are confident that **Aquila and her descendants will be the dominant force in the Armed Forces for many decades to come**.

> Regards,
> Tatiana Kiralova
> Head of Battle Engine Program
> Army Research Division
