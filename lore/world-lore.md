# The World of Allium - In-Game Lore

### Setting

Battle Engine Aquila takes place on an Earth-like planet called **Allium** in the **Porrum system**.

> Over recent decades, dramatic changes to the climate have caused the sea level to rise severely. The rising waters have reduced landmasses considerably and packed the population into ever-decreasing habitable areas. Land is scarce and, as such, a valuable commodity.

Only **13 islands** scattered across the surface remain. The entire game takes place on islands due to **engine limitations** (not story choice).

### The Vegetable Naming Scheme

The developers had fun with Latin botanical names:

| Name | Meaning | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Allium** | Latin for onion/garlic family | The planet |
| **Porrum** | Latin for leek | The star system |
| **Ampeloprasum Archipelago** | Wild leek species name | Also Latin for **Brussels Sprouts** (confirmed by Alex Trowers) - *Note: In real-world botany, Ampeloprasum is actually wild leek (Allium family), not Brussels sprouts (Brassica family). This may be intentional worldbuilding or a developer joke.* |

### The Two Civilizations

Before the waters rose, the **Ampeloprasum Archipelago** was historically occupied by two races:

#### The Forseti

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Territory** | Islands from Forseti Major to Castellian |
| **Character** | Peaceful, technologically advanced |
| **Terrain** | Well-cultivated, fertile islands with farms and townships |
| **Technology** | Clean energy (fusion, solar, wind), plasma weapons, Mag Thrusters, hover vehicles |
| **Philosophy** | Saw environmental changes coming and prepared |
| **Naming Origin** | Norse god of justice/reconciliation |

**Technology Details**:
> Forseti technology is generally considered to be far more advanced than Muspell equivalents. A lot of resources have been ploughed into clean energy research and several advances have been made in the fields of fusion, solar and wind power sources.

> Most Forseti ground vehicles use **hover engines** for mobility although the Muspell has long since stolen this technology and have retro-fitted it into the majority of their own units.

> Forseti aircraft use **Mag Thrusters**, which are cleaner and safer than Jet engines although they do tend to lack the peak power output. This lack of power is never really apparent as lighter composite alloys that go to make the chassis and armour sections mean that the overall weight of the machine tends to be a lot lighter than a Muspell counterpart with more power.

> Weapons in the Forseti Armed Forces are also of the clean variety. Rather than explosives and shells, **plasma energy** is used. It can be just as destructive but it is cleaner to produce and store.

#### The Muspell

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Territory** | Islands from Iron Isle to Sentinel |
| **Character** | Aggressive, militaristic |
| **Terrain** | Largely arid islands |
| **Technology** | Traditional explosives/shells, jet engines, stolen hover tech |
| **Philosophy** | Believe in strength and survival at any cost |
| **Soldiers** | **Genetically engineered** to enhance physical abilities - they feel no pain and know no fear |
| **Naming Origin** | Muspelheim (realm of fire in Norse mythology) |
| **Design** | Jagged, authoritative styling - heavier/bulkier military units |

#### Faction Design Philosophy

The UI designer "really went to town" creating custom **alphabets and languages** for each faction:
- **Muspell**: Jagged, authoritative styling - heavier/bulkier military units
- **Forseti**: Curved, thinner line work - flatter, streamlined units

Norse mythology was raided for naming conventions throughout.

### The Sohra Treaty

> Their history has long been dotted with skirmishes, conflicts and all out wars but the **Sohra Treaty of 1174** was hoped to put an end to hostilities. It set aside the island of **Kensor** to be a demilitarised no-go zone to both races.

> Of course, the increasing necessity for dry land has meant that the old treaties are looking shakier and shakier and it's only a matter of time before tensions will once again result in war...

### The Story

The player assumes the role of **Hawk Winter**, a Forseti dockworker and illicit dock racer. After one of the races, **Tatiana Kiralova** (a friend and overseer of the Aquila program) persuades **Colonel Chuck Kramer** (leader of the Forseti Air Force) to secretly assign Hawk to pilot the Battle Engine.

War erupts with the Muspell attacking RI-04 island. The player wipes out the whole Muspell strike force in time to fight the rest of the war.

**Story Assessment** (from the post-mortem):
> "Too much story, too little script. The storyline went through many revisions... constant editing removed many of the interesting twists... resulted in a faint shadow of what the final plot could have been."

Alex Trowers described the story as "one of the weakest components" — born from technical necessity rather than creative vision.
