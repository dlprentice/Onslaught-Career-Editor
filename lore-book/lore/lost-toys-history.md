Lost Toys Ltd. was a British video game developer founded in March 1999 by Glenn Corpes, Jeremy Longley, and Darran Thomas, all former Bullfrog Productions employees. Les Edgar provided initial financial support.

### The Bullfrog Exodus

Lost Toys was the **third** Bullfrog break-off group, formed due to disillusionment after Electronic Arts purchased Bullfrog:

| Studio | Founders | Philosophy |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Mucky Foot Productions** | Guy Simmons, Mike Diskett, Fin McGechie | Their take on Bullfrog |
| **Lionhead Studios** | Peter Molyneux | Molyneux's take on Bullfrog |
| **Lost Toys** | Glenn Corpes, Jeremy Longley, Darran Thomas | Corpes's take on Bullfrog |

### Why They Left - In Their Own Words

**Darran Thomas** (Director, Head of Art):
> "The working culture changed considerably after EA's takeover."

**Glenn Corpes** (Director, Technical Director):
> "It was quite embarrassing to still be working for the Borg."

> "Lost Toys was partially my take on what Bullfrog was, as opposed to Mucky Foot (Simmons/Diskett/McGechie's take) and Lionhead (Molyneux's take)."

**Alex Trowers** (Designer):
> "People were leaving because it was EA, not Bullfrog. We intended to go back to developing games for its own sake rather than to keep shareholders satisfied."

**Jeremy Longley** (Managing Director):
> Small teams were "valuable to the gaming industry" and could "work creatively like Bullfrog." It was "encouraging to see Mucky Foot and Lionhead break off from Bullfrog successfully."

### Company Philosophy

From their website profile:

> "The company's mission is to put the fun back into the games industry with unique and innovative games."

> "Their extensive experience taught them that fun, innovative games can be more successful than the 'me too' titles of their rivals."

> "We're going to bring playability and originality back into focus with our game designs. Much of the industry nowadays is about doing exactly the same thing as your competitors but trying to be five percent better. There is still room for true, groundbreaking creativity, and we're going to prove it!" — Glenn Corpes

### MoHo / Ball Breakers

Lost Toys' first title was released under Take Two's Rockstar label in August 2000:

> "We are very impressed with both the design and the style of the game from Lost Toys. With its Bullfrog heritage, the studio has the capacity to create fresh, original product. As ever, we strive to publish the best of the best at Take Two, and believe we have found that working with Lost Toys." — Kelly Sumner, European President of Take Two

> "It's a cool little game... inspired by Marble Madness." — Glenn Corpes

> "MoHo is a next-generation game on a now-generation platform." — Jeremy Longley (April 2000)

The minimal violence was in line with Bullfrog's philosophy of making games targeted toward all gamers.

### The Stunt Car Racer Partnership

In May 2003, **Geoff Crammond** (creator of the original Stunt Car Racer and the Grand Prix series) chose Lost Toys as his company Simergy's partner to develop **Stunt Car Racer Pro**. Lost Toys would be responsible for art and graphics.

> "Geoff chose Lost Toys because of their proven track record of producing great multiformat titles on time and to budget."

Public recollection recorded in June 2022:
> "Arghh yes that correct. Stunt car racer was the next game, and yes got to working demo, but never got released."

Historical clarification: the "working demo" phrasing applies to **Stunt Car Racer Pro**, not to Battle Engine Aquila retail.

Crammond's business manager later called it "the best game he never sold."

### Closure

Lost Toys closed on **2 October 2003**, with all staff made redundant. The studio was affected by the dot-com bust and failed to secure a publisher for post-BEA projects.

Additional historical note: there were no active sequel plans for BEA during the original release/closure period.

The team scattered — some still in games, some moved on entirely. The original directors (Glenn Corpes, Jeremy Longley) hadn't been in regular contact with each other for 20+ years until Glenn joined the BEA Discord in April 2025 after Stuart invited him.
